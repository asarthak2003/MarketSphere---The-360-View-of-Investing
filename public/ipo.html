Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is an IPO?

An Initial Public Offering (IPO) is the process by which a private company becomes a public company by offering its shares to the public for the first time. The company gets listed on a stock exchange.

2. Why do companies go for an IPO?

Companies go public to raise capital for expansion, pay off debt, provide an exit for early investors (like venture capitalists), or to gain public recognition and credibility.

3. What is a Price Band?

In a book-built issue, the company provides a price range (e.g., Rs 100 - Rs 110) within which investors can bid. The final price, or "Cut-off Price," is determined after the bidding process is complete.

4. How can one apply for an IPO online?

You can apply for an IPO through your brokerage account using the ASBA (Application Supported by Blocked Amount) facility. Your bank account is linked, and the application amount is blocked (not debited) until shares are allotted.

5. What is the benefit of an IPO?

For investors, it's an opportunity to buy shares of a company at an early stage, potentially benefiting from "listing gains" if the stock opens at a higher price, or from long-term growth.

6. What are the disadvantages of an IPO?

There is no guarantee of listing gains; the stock price can open lower than the issue price. Also, allotment is not guaranteed, especially for oversubscribed IPOs, as it's often done via a lottery system.

7. What is the difference between book building and fixed price issue?

In a fixed price issue, the company sets a single, fixed price for its shares. In a book-building issue (more common), the company provides a price band, and investors bid within that range. The final price is discovered based on demand.

8. Is it mandatory to have a PAN number to apply in an IPO?

Yes, a valid PAN (Permanent Account Number) is mandatory for all financial transactions in India, including applying for an IPO. Your PAN must be linked to your Demat and bank accounts.

9. Where do I get an IPO application form?

You don't need a physical form. You can apply online through your stockbroker's app or website (like Zerodha, Upstox) or through your net banking portal (e.g., HDFC, ICICI) via the ASBA facility.

10. Can a minor apply in an IPO?

A minor cannot apply for an IPO directly. However, an adult (guardian) can apply on behalf of a minor, provided the minor has a Demat account and a bank account in their name.

11. What is the procedure to withdraw from an IPO?

Yes, you can withdraw your IPO bid before the bidding window closes. You can do this through the same platform (broker or bank) you used to apply. The blocked funds will be released.

12. How is IPO return calculated?

IPO return is often first calculated as "listing gain/loss": ((Listing Price - Issue Price) / Issue Price) * 100. Any returns after the listing day are calculated just like any other stock based on the current market price (CMP).

13. What is the minimum and maximum investment one could do in the HNI category?

The HNI (High Net-worth Individual) category, also known as the NII (Non-Institutional Investor) category, is for applications above ₹2 lakhs. The minimum application is just over ₹2 lakhs, and there is generally no upper limit, though it can be restricted by the total issue size reserved for the HNI quota.

14. How to apply more than one application in an IPO?

You cannot submit multiple applications from the same PAN card. This will result in all your applications being rejected. However, you can submit separate applications from the accounts of your different family members (e.g., spouse, parents), each using their own unique PAN and Demat account.

15. What is RHP and DRHP?

DRHP stands for Draft Red Herring Prospectus. It's the preliminary registration document filed with SEBI, containing details about the company's business, financials, and the IPO, but not the final price or issue date. RHP stands for Red Herring Prospectus. It's the updated version of the DRHP, which includes the price band and issue opening/closing dates.
